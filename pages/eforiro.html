Nigerian Eforiro Soup

Description

Efo riro (Yoruba: ẹ̀fọ́ riro) is a vegetable soup and a native soup of the Yoruba people of western Nigeria.The two vegetables most commonly used to prepare the soup are Celosia argentea (Efo ṣọkọ̀) and Amaranthus hybridus (ẹ̀fọ́ tẹ̀tẹ̀).

Ingredients

  • Beef
  • Cow tripe (shaki)
  • Cow skin (ponmo)
  • Smoked fish
  • Dried fish
  • Palm oil
  • Celosia argentea or green amaranth
  • Peppers (red bell peppers or chili peppers)
  • crayfish
  • Onions
  • Bouillon cubes
  • Locust beans (irú)
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Pick the vegetable from the stalk and slice into chunks.
  2. Rinse vegetable clean, drain and set aside.
  3. Wash locust beans and set aside.
  4. Chop 1 onion and set aside.
  5. Shred the smoked fish into flakes and also set aside.
  6. Roughly chop the bell pepper (tatashe) and onions, add to a blender and pulse for coarse texture (alternatively, use a food processor for perfect coarse texture).
  7. Place a pan on medium heat and add palm oil, heat for about 5 minutes but don’t bleach , add half of the locust beans, chopped onions and fry in the oil for about 2 minutes . Add blended pepper to the palm oil and stir to combine. After about 3 minutes, add the remaining locust beans. Move on to add the cow skin and ground crayfish and cook for another 3-4 minutes on low heat.
  8. Add seasoning and pinch of salt. (check for salt at the later stage of the cooking) Whilst you are waiting for the sauce to cook, boil water enough to cover the vegetable. Once the water is boiled, pour over the vegetable and cover with a lid for 5-10 minutes. Strain and squeeze out excess water from the vegetable.
  9. Continue to cook the sauce till oil floats on the top, add half of the smoked fish, dried smelt fish and stir to combine. (You can add little water to the sauce if it is too dry but do not add too much as efo riro is almost water free) Add the vegetable to the sauce and stir till all is well incorporated.
  10. dd the remaining smoked mackerel and carefully combine in the vegetable. Check for salt and cook for another 4 minutes. Take it off the heat and set aside. Serve efo riro soup with a swallow of choice or rice and you can even choose to eat it on its own.

Cullinary Accompaniments

Ẹ̀fọ́ riro is often eaten with Nigerian swallow foods like eba, pounded yam, or fufu. Other starchy foods such as rice, rice & beans and boiled plantain may also be eaten with it.
