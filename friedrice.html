Nigerian Fried rice

Description

Fried rice has been a kitchen staple since as early as the Sui Dynasty (589â€“618 CE) in China. The primary reason for the continued popularity and ubiquity of this dish comes down to two things: its adaptability and the fact that people almost always cook too much rice to eat in one sitting.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp. sesame oil, divided
  • large eggs
  • salt
  • 2 tablespoon oil.
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced, white and green parts divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp. peeled and minced ginger (from a 1" piece)
  • 2 cups cooked long grain rice
  • 3/4 c. frozen peas
  • 3 tbsp. low-sodium soy sauce

Instructions

  1. Heat a pot over high heat until very hot, about 2 minutes. Add 1 tablespoon oil.
  2. Beat egg with 2 teaspoons water and a large pinch salt and add to the pot. Cook, stirring to form large soft curds, about 30 seconds. Transfer to a plate.
  3. Return pot to high heat and add 2 tablespoons oil, the carrots, and whites of the green onions. Cook until lightly golden, about 2 minutes. Add garlic and ginger and cook, stirring, until fragrant, 1 minute.
  4. Add rice, peas, and cooked eggs to skillet. Pour in soy sauce and cook, stirring until heated through, 1 minute. Season with salt and pepper and stir in the remaining green onions.
